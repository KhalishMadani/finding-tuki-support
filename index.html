Finding Tuki

A pixel-art endless runner for iOS where a Mom Turtle swims a polluted ocean to find her lost baby — Tuki. Collect glowing jellyfish, dodge the trash humans leave behind, and swim as far as the tide will carry you.

📱 iOS 🕹️ Endless Runner 🎨 Pixel Art 🌐 EN / ID

🌊 About the Game

You play Mom Turtle, swimming up-screen through a scrolling ocean. The sea is beautiful — and full of human garbage. Weave through trash bags, plastic bottles, six-pack rings, and sea mines, snack on jellyfish to keep your energy meter alive, and chase the one thing that matters: your baby, Tuki.

Beneath the arcade loop is a gentle message about ocean pollution and the everyday hazards marine life faces. Everything you dodge is something humans threw away.

🎮 How to Play

🫲 Swipe left / rightSteer the turtle
🪼 Swim into jellyfishRefill your energy meter
🗑️ 🍶 ⭕ 💣 HazardsDodge them — one hit and you're in trouble
⏸️ Pause buttonPause the game, open settings

Goal: survive as long as possible. Your energy drains constantly, jellyfish refill it, and the ocean scrolls faster the longer you last. Beat your best score and climb the leaderboard.

❓ FAQ

The leaderboard is empty or won't load.
The leaderboard is powered by Game Center. Make sure you're signed in: open Settings → Games (Game Center) on your device and sign in with your Apple Account, then relaunch the game. Scores submit automatically after each run once you're signed in.
Is my score saved if I lose internet connection?
Yes. Your best score and run history are saved on your device the moment a run ends. You never lose a score to a bad connection.
How do I turn off music, sound effects, or vibration?
Open the in-game Settings menu (from the pause screen or main menu). Music and sound effects have separate volume sliders, and haptics (vibration) has its own toggle.
How do I change the language?
Finding Tuki supports English and Indonesian. Switch between them in the in-game Settings menu — no need to change your device language.
Can I replay the tutorial?
The guided tutorial runs automatically on your first play. If you'd like to see it again, reinstalling the app resets the tutorial flag — or just email us and we'll help you out.
Does the game collect my personal data?
The game stores your scores and settings on your device. Online leaderboard features use Apple's Game Center, which is governed by Apple's own privacy policy. We don't run our own accounts, and there are no ads or third-party trackers.
Which devices are supported?
Finding Tuki runs on iPhone and iPad on recent versions of iOS / iPadOS. If the game misbehaves on your device, email us with your device model and iOS version and we'll investigate.
